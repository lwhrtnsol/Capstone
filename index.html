Professional Self-Assessment

I am completing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a concentration in Project Management at Southern New Hampshire University. I entered the program with the goal of moving from automotive production into a more technical or project-focused career. That goal has remained the same, but my understanding of the work has grown. I now see how software development, data systems, cybersecurity, testing, and project coordination all work together to support reliable business operations.

My background in manufacturing has influenced the way I approach technology. At Toyota, I have seen the importance of quality, safety, communication, standardized work, and continuous improvement. The Computer Science program gave me the technical foundation to connect those experiences with software and data-driven solutions. I have learned to look beyond whether an application works and ask whether it is secure, maintainable, understandable, and useful to the people who depend on it.

My ePortfolio demonstrates that growth through three enhanced artifacts. The Travlr Getaways application demonstrates my ability to work across a full-stack system by improving validation, API error handling, secure configuration, and dynamic trip rendering. The SSL Server demonstrates my understanding of algorithms through SHA-256 hashing, byte-array processing, input validation, and structured responses. The ChrisMitchellInventory application demonstrates database skills through SQLite table design, foreign key relationships, migration logic, validation, and user-specific inventory records.

Together, these artifacts demonstrate the Computer Science program outcomes by showing my ability to use current development tools, evaluate design choices, apply algorithmic principles, improve database-driven applications, and approach software with a security mindset. They also show my ability to communicate technical decisions clearly through written narratives and a code review. Each enhancement required me to review existing work, identify weaknesses, make focused improvements, test the results, and explain why the changes matter.

My next career step is to transition into a technical, project-focused, or operations support role where I can contribute to software, data, process improvement, and business systems. I plan to continue developing my technical skills while gaining project experience and pursuing professional certifications such as CAPM. This ePortfolio represents both the work I have completed in the program and the direction I intend to take in my career.
